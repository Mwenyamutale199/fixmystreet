About Lusaka Ward

Welcome to Lusaka Ward

Lusaka Ward is a vibrant community nestled in the heart of Lusaka. With a rich history and diverse population, our ward is dedicated to fostering a strong sense of belonging and community spirit.

Our ward is known for its commitment to community engagement and sustainable development initiatives. We believe in empowering our residents to actively participate in shaping the future of our ward.

Our Values

At Lusaka Ward, we prioritize the well-being of our residents. Here are some of our core values:

Healthy Living

We promote healthy living by encouraging regular exercise and consumption of nutritious foods. Prioritize your health by incorporating physical activity and balanced meals into your daily routine.

Community Engagement

Engaging with our community is essential for fostering a sense of belonging and unity. Participate in local events, volunteer initiatives, and community projects to contribute positively to Lusaka Ward.

Sustainability

We are committed to preserving our environment and promoting sustainable practices. Reduce, reuse, and recycle to minimize waste and protect the natural beauty of Lusaka Ward for future generations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions about Lusaka:

Lusaka offers a variety of attractions, including the Lusaka National Museum, Munda Wanga Environmental Park, and the Sunday Crafts Market.

The best time to visit Lusaka is during the dry season, from May to October, when the weather is cooler and wildlife viewing is optimal.

Lusaka is generally safe for tourists, but like any major city, it's important to exercise caution and be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

Lusaka offers various transportation options, including taxis, minibusses (known as minibuses), and car rentals. It's advisable to negotiate fares with taxi drivers beforehand.

Visitors to Lusaka should try local delicacies such as nshima (a maize-based staple), kapenta (small freshwater fish), and chikanda (a traditional snack made from wild orchid tubers).

Yes, Lusaka hosts several cultural festivals throughout the year, including the Lusaka International Film Festival, Zambia International Trade Fair, and Lusaka July (a horse racing event).
